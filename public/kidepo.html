About Kidepo Valley National Park

Nestled in the rugged, semi-arid valleys of Uganda’s northeastern border, Kidepo Valley National Park is a true hidden gem. Known for its stunning landscapes and vast, untouched wilderness, Kidepo offers some of Africa’s most breathtaking scenery. This remote park is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts, featuring over 77 mammal species, including lions, elephants, buffaloes, and the elusive cheetah, along with more than 475 bird species. With sweeping savannahs, dramatic mountains, and the vibrant cultures of the Karamojong and Ik tribes, Kidepo Valley promises a unique and authentic African safari experience.

Activites

Game Drives:

Explore the park’s open savannahs and spot lions, giraffes, and herds of buffalo.

Nature Walks:

Enjoy guided walks through the park’s diverse landscapes, including the Narus Valley and Kanangorok Hot Springs.

Cultural Encounters:

Engage with the local Karamojong and Ik communities to learn about their traditions and way of life.

Bird Watching:

Discover a wide range of bird species, including the majestic Abyssinian Ground Hornbill.

Scenic Views:

Capture the stunning views of the Morungole Mountains and the expansive Kidepo Valley.
